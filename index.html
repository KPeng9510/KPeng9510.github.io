Dr.-Ing. Kunyu Peng

Post-Doctoral Researcher · CVHCI Lab · Karlsruhe Institute of Technology

I work on deep learning-based multi-modal video understanding and trustworthy human-centric AI, with focus on language-guided video understanding (referring atomic action recognition and referring human action segmentation in multi-person scenarios), open-set skeleton-based action recognition, open-set domain generalization, label noise learning, and multi-modal large language models (MLLMs). My methodological contributions include cross-modal uncertainty-aware logits calibration with channel-normalized distance refinement (CrossMax) for open-set action recognition, cross-stream agent attention over video, text, and location-semantic streams for referring atomic action recognition (RefAtomNet / RefAtomNet++), holistic–partial Fourier-conditioned diffusion with bidirectional cross-input gated HP-xLSTM for fine-grained temporal action segmentation (HopaDIFF), evidential bi-level hardest-domain scheduling in meta-learning for open-set domain generalization, and hyperbolic space category prototypes with prompt augmentation for hierarchical representation learning under noisy labels.

Looking forward, I am pursuing interdisciplinary research at the intersection of MLLMs, video understanding, motion generation and editing, and trustworthy AI, including multi-robot collaborative video perception and reasoning with uncertainty communication and life-long shared memory banks, EgoPano human-centric collaborative reasoning that combines panoramic and egocentric views for cyber-physical situation awareness, MLLM-based online coaching for first-aid and rehabilitation for users without prior experience, and unified text-guided models for multi-human 3D motion generation, editing, and understanding — building on our InterEdit dataset for text-guided multi-human motion editing. My applications span rehabilitation, first-aid training, assistive systems for visually impaired individuals, digital aging, cyber-physical security monitoring, and human-robot cooperation in smart homes and circular factory environments.

About

I received my PhD in Informatics from Karlsruhe Institute of Technology in 2024, where my dissertation focused on Towards Generalizable Deep Learning-Based Human Action Recognition. Before that, I completed my MSc in Electrical Engineering and Information Technology at KIT and a BSc in Automation at Beijing Institute of Technology.

My broader goal is to build robust, generalizable, and trustworthy multi-modal perception systems that reason about human behavior across domains, sensors, and deployment conditions — with applications in healthcare, rehabilitation, assistive technologies, and human-robot cooperation.

Research Interests

My research lies in deep learning-based multi-modal video understanding and trustworthy challenges in computer vision, including label noise learning, domain generalization, and open-world recognition. I develop methodologies that address multi-modal interaction tasks and close the gap between training distributions and real-world deployment conditions.

My key methodological contributions include:

  • Cross-modal uncertainty-aware logits calibration to improve awareness of unseen classes for open-set action recognition.
  • Text-guided holistic-partial Fourier-conditioned diffusion networks for human action segmentation in multi-person scenarios.
  • Text-guided atomic video action recognition using cross-stream agent attention.
  • Bi-level hardest-domain scheduling in meta-learning for advanced open-set domain generalization.
  • Hyperbolic space prototypes for label denoising in domain-agnostic meta-learning under noisy labels.

Future Research Directions

My future agenda focuses on interdisciplinary advancements at the intersection of multi-modal large language models (MLLMs), video understanding, motion generation and understanding, and trustworthy AI, with applications in rehabilitation, first-aid training, assistance for visually impaired individuals, and digital aging:

  • Uncertainty-aware multi-robot collaborative spatial reasoning and human activity understanding — modeling uncertainty propagation across multiple cooperating robots via structured communication, multimodal fusion, and hyperbolic memory graphs for reliable long-term spatial knowledge.
  • Trustworthy human action-aware MLLMs for cybersecurity, first-aid, and rehabilitation — joint reasoning across egocentric and exocentric perspectives, interactive multimodal training assistants, and parameter-efficient tuning with evidential uncertainty estimation.
  • Text-guided multi-human interaction, action editing, and understanding — MLLM- and diffusion-based frameworks for multi-human motion understanding, editing, and generation with interaction-aware multimodal representations.
